Rhea
(/ˈriːə/; Ancient Greek: Ῥέα [r̥é.aː] or Ῥεία [r̥ěː.aː])


A relief depicting Rhea handing out the stone to Cronus, Capitoline Museum

Rhea, also known as Rhia or Rheia, was a titan in Greek Mythology. Rhea was one of the daughters of Uranus, the sky deity
and the earth deity Gaia. Through her marriage with Cronus, her brother and husband, Gaia birthed the deities Hestia, Demeter, Hera, Hades, Poseidon and Zeus.
Rhea is known for saving her youngest son, Zeus, from being devoured by Cronus, who, out of fear of being overthrown by one of his children, swallowed them all.
She saved Zeus by hiding him in Crete and presented to Cronus a rock swaddled in cloth instead of his youngest son.
Zeus would eventually defeat his father which led to his siblings being disgorged.
